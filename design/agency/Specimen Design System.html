SPECIMEN · DESIGN SYSTEM · v1.0 · 2026-04-16

A field manual for alien anatomy.

Specimen is a mobile logic puzzle. The player is a xenobiologist. This is the visual system — tone, palette, type, organs, channels, components, motion — for the repair console the player operates. The look sits in the seam between a 17th-century anatomical plate and a deep-space containment lab. The only saturated light in the room is bioluminescence.

01 · PRINCIPLESi.
The three rules the whole system obeys.

The creature is the interface.

No tooltips, no tutorial, no kill-screen text that explains why something failed. Everything the player learns is inferred from how the specimen responds. Pixels serve that.

01
Dim is default, light means life.
The chamber is near-black. Saturated color only appears where something is alive — a healthy organ, a running channel, a revival glow. Damage = dim. Never the other way round.
02
Legibility over literalism.
The creature is alien, not chaotic. Silhouettes, organs, and channels read at a glance on a 5-inch portrait screen. Monospace labels sit under every diagram — we treat the UI as a specimen tag, not a game HUD.
03
Failure is informative.
A wrong answer isn't a punishment screen. It's data. Organ rejection flashes the offending slots only; structural failure claims the whole specimen. The player learns which by watching.
02 · COLORii.
Locked brief colors (do not shift) plus the agency-filled palette.

Palette.

Five colors carry meaning: two greens, a red, a violet, a yellow. All other color is surface. The palette is calibrated so bioluminescence reads instantly against a near-black chamber.

05 · CREATUREv.
Vorrkai IV — annotated wireframe, agency to replace.

The specimen.

The silhouette is a blueprint placeholder — bilateral, slot-symmetric, centrally anchored. Final form is the agency's call. All we fix: 80% screen width; a fade-in from dim; four slot anchors at compass points.

06 · ORGANSvi.
Four types · two states · one rule of thumb per organ.

Four organs, four jobs.

Each organ has a single function the player will eventually recognize by silhouette alone. Treatments shown below are placeholders — the agency delivers the final sprites. A damaged variant keeps the identity hue, dims it, and distorts the silhouette.

// DAMAGED STATE · RULE

A damaged organ must still be recognizable — we never shift hue. We reduce luminance, break the silhouette, and let the health tick flip red. The player should feel, without reading, that something is wrong.

07 · CHANNELSvii.
Two channel types. Two weights. Two rhythms.

PULSE and FLUID.

Channels are how organs talk. A PULSE is a bioelectric signal — fast, thin, sparky. A FLUID is organic matter in motion — slow, viscous, broad. Weight and rhythm do all the teaching; the player never needs a legend.

// ROUTING

MVP uses straight lines; V1 uses curved organic routes with bezier control offsets on the perpendicular to the slot-pair axis. Curvature bends toward the creature's centerline for FLUID, and away for PULSE — so the two never overlap visually.

// SLOT FRAME · 3 STATES
SLOT RULES
  • Corner brackets always visible — they are the slot's identity.
  • Empty state: dashed inset rect, "SLOT S1" label, muted ink-quaternary.
  • Selected target (empty + an organ is in-hand): yellow dashed ring, 5-4 dash, slow spin.
  • Filled: brackets + ring adopt organ identity hue. Health tick in top-right.
  • Failing: brackets flash organ-red three times at 150ms period, then dim.

09 · MOTIONix.
Three outcomes. Three rhythms. Zero ambiguity.

The creature speaks in three sequences.

Every timing below is Godot-Tween-compatible. V1 particle upgrades replace the glow plates but keep the choreography. Durations are budgeted generously — these are the only moments the whole game stops to say something.

Revival
~2.00s · satisfying, restrained
  1. 010.10sSoft white pulse from creature center · ease-out
  2. 020.72sEach slot blooms to biolum-green in sequence · 180ms interval
  3. 03holdCreature settles on pale-green tint · chamber saturate +10%
  4. 04—RUN is replaced by CONTINUE · result area reads "Specimen repaired"
Organ rejection
~1.50s · sharp, local, diagnostic
  1. 010.00sAll wrong slots flash organ-red simultaneously · correct slots untouched
  2. 020.45sThree 150ms flash cycles between #FF3333 and #5A1414
  3. 030.50sResidual red tint lingers on failed slots, then clears
  4. 04—Chamber 3-frame shake · result area: "Organ misfit — rejection detected"
Structural failure
~2.50s · dramatic, whole-specimen
  1. 010.20sChamber dims 60% — black overlay crossfade
  2. 020.00sAll slots flash structural-violet simultaneously
  3. 030.30s"STRUCTURAL FAILURE" in Spectral italic fades in · violet glow
  4. 041.00sHolds · subtitle "// SUBJECT COHERENCE LOST" in mono
  5. 050.50sOverlay and text fade · board returns to idle
